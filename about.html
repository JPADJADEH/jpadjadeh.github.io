About Me

John-Paul Adjadeh

Ph.D. Candidate | Governance & Political Institutions | Policy & Research

John-Paul Adjadeh is a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at The University of Texas at Dallas, specializing in governance, political institutions, and state stability. He holds a B.A. in Social Work with Geography and Resource Development and an MPhil in Public Administration from the University of Ghana, Legon.

With over a decade of experience as Principal Registrar at Ghana’s Ministry of Chieftaincy and Religious Affairs, John-Paul has worked extensively on governance, institutional reforms, and conflict resolution. His research explores the interaction between legal systems, democratic stability, and state repression, with several papers under review and conference presentations.

He is a Brian J.L. Berry Scholar, a recipient of the 2024-2025 Government and Political Science Fellowship, and a member of Phi Kappa Phi. He also serves as a reviewer for the Journal of Emerging Investigators and is an Associate with the Program on Governance and Local Development.

John-Paul’s work bridges academia and policy, aiming to advance governance reforms, institutional development, and sustainable conflict resolution.
